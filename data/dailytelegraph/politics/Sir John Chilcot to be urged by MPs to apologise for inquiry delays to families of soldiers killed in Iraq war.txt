Bob Neill, the chairman of the Justice committee, said he would like to see “an explanation for the delay at the very least.

“It has been a very long delay and people would like to know why that is in a bit more detail than we have had so far.”

He added that it was “important to recognise the distress caused” by delays to the inquiry which began in 2009 and was meant to take two years.

He said that “maybe there are lessons to be learned about keeping people in the loop” on long running inquiries.

The report – which ran to 2.6 million words and cost over £10.3million – was eventually published in July.
